lil prah

lil prah tells us a the story of how skating came to be. Surfers in the 50's looking for a way to spend the flat days, found their insipration in rollerskating. Strapping rollerskate trucks to pieces of drift wood they learned how to translate their movements to the street - not much different as to how the acient hawaiians learned to surf on drift wood. It was about flow, turns and feeling out what the board told you to do.

lil prah lets you experience the 50's, the first carves, skating barefoot.

21" deck

The tiny deck - or "sidewalk skateboard" - made of a hollowed piece of oak wood. Extremely durable, but lightweight thanks to the hollowed out core. Rounded edges and a linseed oil finish. You'll be suprised by how grippy it is and as a bonus, super comfortable for a barefoot skate.

  • 21" flat oak deck
  • hollowed core and rounded edges
  • linseed oil finished

tiny trucks

The first skateboards sported 'cut in half' rollerskate trucks bolted onto a piece of drift wood. After it became apperent that the trucks couldnt handle the load these surfers where putting onto them, the first dedicated skate trucks appeared.

Our trucks sport the super narrow hangers, but have the durability of todays standards.

planet-friendly wheels

Every single skateboard wheel is made from poly-urathane. It's an incredible material: its cheap, easy to work with and lasts a lifetime. However our planet has had enough of all the garbage and so do we. Nowadays we can make any type if plastic from fungi. With almost all of the amazing properties of conventional plastics, except after it has served its purpose you can burry it in your garden and a plant will grow out if it.

It has been a long and painfull process, but we have succesfully developed fungi based wheels for lil prah.
